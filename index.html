About Me

Hey! So, what's so great about me that you would want to come to this random website and read about someone you've never met? Well, I'll let you decide that. In all reality, I am not a very excting person. I am very much a homebody, and would prefer to sit around and read or watch a good movie than go and party all night. I know...like I said, I am not a very exciting person. But, since you came to my "About Me" page, I guess I had better tell you a little about myself. Here we go. I am 29 years old and have lived in Utah my whole life. Yep, I'm that person. I live in a cute little town called Erda, which is neslted in Tooele County. I love it here! There's farm land and mountains, not a lot of people, and the view is amazing. I have two beautiful girls, one is six and just started first grade this year, and I have a four year old who thinks she is going on 18. It's one heck of a ride, but I wouldn't have it any other way.

How did I get interested in code? That is a story in and of itself. No, I'm kidding. Once again, I'm boring, so this story isn't going to be very exciting. So, Once Upon a Time, I was trying to get onto a website, but one of the buttons wouldn't work, which was then preventing me from continuing my registration on this particular website, so I right clicked the screen and saw "Inspect" at the bottom. Not knowing what this was, I clicked on it, and it brought me into a scramble of letters. I realized that this was code and was telling the website what to do. I found the code for the button I was trying to use and saw that the function was disabled, so I changed the wording, hoping this would get it to work. Low and behold...it worked! I was so excited. I literally got up and did a little jig. At that moment I didn't think, "Hmmm, maybe I should become a web developer", I honestly don't remember my exact thought, but I know it wasn't that. It wasn't until a year later that I was driving through Draper, Utah and saw a ton of billboards advertising work for coding. I couldn't stop thinking about it, and a few months later, I started researching places that taught code. I needed something that was cheap, so I started with FreeCodingCamp. It was informational, but I felt like I wasn't really committed, and I wasn't learning in the best way. And due to my recent divorce, I needed something that would allow me to have reliable work that also paid well. That's when I started researching actual schools. As I was searching, the first search query that I found was for the University of Utah Coding Bootcamp. I looked into it, started talking to some couselors, and long story short...here I am, learning to code like a boss!
